## Key Observations

1. **Hyperspectral gap is significant**: EnMAP, PRISMA, DESIS, and EMIT together provide operational imaging spectroscopy that was completely absent from the initial review. These are critical for mineral mapping, vegetation biochemistry, and soil property estimation.

2. **Soil moisture has dedicated missions**: SMOS and SMAP are the two flagship L-band soil moisture missions -- neither was in the original catalog. ESA CCI Soil Moisture provides the longest merged record (1978-present).

3. **Precipitation is deeply covered**: GPM/IMERG, TRMM, CHIRPS, GPCC, MSWEP, and E-OBS collectively provide precipitation at multiple scales, sources, and latencies. GPM IMERG (0.1 deg, 30-min) is the current gold standard.

4. **Free weather APIs are transformative**: Open-Meteo and Pirate Weather provide zero-barrier access to operational weather data that previously required institutional agreements. Open-Meteo in particular is production-ready with no API key required.

5. **New missions are operational**: NISAR (launched Jul 2025, operational Jan 2026) and Biomass (launched Apr 2025, open data Jan 2026) are already delivering data. FLEX (fluorescence) launches Sep 2026.

6. **Hydrology framework is mature**: HydroSHEDS/HydroBASINS + HydroLAKES + GLWD + GRanD form a complete global hydrographic framework. All free, CC-BY licensed, and interoperable.

7. **Methane monitoring is multi-tiered**: TROPOMI (already in review) + MethaneSAT + GHGSat + EMIT provide coverage from global screening to facility-level attribution.
